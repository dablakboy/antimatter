How to Install / Sideload on iOS

Sideloading means installing apps (IPA files) on your iPhone/iPad outside of the official App Store — usually for tweaked, beta, or custom apps.

Here are the most popular & reliable methods in 2025/2026 (no jailbreak required for most):

  • Sideloadly — Free computer-based tool (Windows/Mac). Connect your device via USB, drag IPA → install. Uses your own Apple ID (free) or paid developer account for longer signing.
  • AltStore / AltStore PAL — Classic & trusted. Requires a computer (Windows/Mac) or works directly on-device with PAL (EU only). Free, but apps refresh every 7 days unless you have a paid Apple Developer account ($99/year).
  • Signulous / AppDB / Scarlet / TrollStore — Online signing services or on-device tools. Some are free, others paid (~$20/year). Very convenient, no computer needed for most.
  • Esign / 3uTools / others — On-device signing apps (often shared via TestFlight or similar). Certificates expire frequently, so you may need to reinstall every few days/weeks.
  • Developer / Enterprise Certificates — Paid services or leaked enterprise certs (risky — can be revoked anytime by Apple).

Recommendation for beginners: Start with Sideloadly (free, reliable) or Scarlet/Esign (on-device, easy).

Important: Sideloading carries some risk — only install IPAs from trusted sources. Always back up your device first.
